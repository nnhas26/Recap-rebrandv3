VOL. 3 - Data Analytics: How Business Decision-making has Changed with Technology and Data

Summary: In this webinar session, Evan explained about data analytics and the benefits of data as the foundation for decision-making. Before getting into data analytics, Evan provides an example of Netflix. Netflix is a movie streaming platform that is also a data-based company. Netflix, which has 200 million subscribers in the world, allows its users to get very personal movie references for each user.

For Netflix users, they must be aware when every time they finish watching a film, there will be a list of movie references based on the movie that has been watched or that are matched with the users’ overall genre preferences. Moreover, Netflix also creates a must-watch movie playlist. This is one of the benefits of the user database.

In addition, every viewer activity data on Netflix is designed to make us happy to linger in the application. Data such as: watching time, scrolling and browsing, the device the audience uses, the rating that the audience gives to the movie, even when the audience pause, rewind, forward, or skip when watching a boring movie, audience data is also being used and analyzed.

From the data, it produces insights for Netflix’s product development. In addition, it also used to produce the fun fact released by Netflix, such as most people watch TV shows or series on weekdays and most people choose to watch feature films on weekends.

According to Evan, analyzing data in business must be done since the business was starting to run. Therefore, entrepreneurs will have the material or foundation to make decisions. Netflix could be as big as it is now because it implemented data analysis since the beginning of the business.

This can be done by collecting simple data, such as buyer/user feedback, areas that use our products/services, what features are used and not used, to the experience felt by the customers. Then after that, entrepreneurs can take the time to analyze the data that has been obtained.
